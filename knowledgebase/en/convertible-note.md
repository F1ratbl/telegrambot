# Convertible Note

Sources:
- SEC Investor.gov - Convertible Securities: https://www.investor.gov/introduction-investing/investing-basics/glossary/convertible-securities
- SEC EDGAR company filings: https://www.sec.gov/search-filings

A convertible note is debt that can be converted into equity under defined terms. It usually pays interest like debt, but gives investors a conversion option if the stock performs well.

Economic interpretation:
- It can provide cheaper financing than straight equity or traditional debt.
- It may create future dilution if converted into shares.
- The coupon, conversion price, maturity, call features, capped call transactions and use of proceeds matter.
- If proceeds fund high-return growth, the market may view it positively. If proceeds cover cash burn or weak liquidity, the market may react negatively.

When explaining company news:
- Mention whether the deal increases cash.
- Explain potential dilution.
- Compare the conversion price with the current stock price.
- Check whether a capped call or hedge reduces dilution risk.
- Avoid saying the news is purely bullish or bearish without looking at terms.
