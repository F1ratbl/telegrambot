# Earnings and Guidance

Sources:
- SEC EDGAR filings: https://www.sec.gov/search-filings
- Company investor relations pages.

Earnings reports show a company's revenue, profitability, margins, cash flow and balance sheet for a period. Guidance is management's outlook for future quarters or the full year.

Market interpretation:
- Stocks usually react to results versus expectations, not only absolute numbers.
- A company can beat current-quarter earnings but fall if guidance disappoints.
- Revenue growth, gross margin, operating margin, free cash flow and backlog/order trends should be reviewed together.

When explaining news:
- Separate actual results from guidance.
- Mention whether the market reaction is likely driven by expectations.
- Avoid saying "good earnings means stock must rise"; valuation and positioning matter.
