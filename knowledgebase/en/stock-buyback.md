# Stock Buyback

Sources:
- SEC company filings: https://www.sec.gov/search-filings
- Company investor relations announcements.

A stock buyback is when a company repurchases its own shares. It can return capital to shareholders and reduce share count.

Economic interpretation:
- Buybacks can support earnings per share.
- They are more attractive when the stock is undervalued and the company has strong free cash flow.
- Buybacks funded with debt or done at excessive valuations may destroy value.

When explaining news:
- Check authorization size relative to market cap.
- Check whether the company actually repurchases shares or only authorizes a plan.
- Compare buybacks with debt levels, capex needs and growth opportunities.
